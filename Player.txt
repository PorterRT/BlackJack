Blackjack, also known as 21, is a popular casino card game where the objective is to beat the dealer's hand without exceeding a total of 21 points. Here's how to play:

Instructions for Blackjack:
The Goal: Reach a hand value as close to 21 as possible without going over, and have a higher total than the dealer.

Card Values:

Number cards count as their face value (2-10).
Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points.
Aces can be worth 1 or 11 points, whichever is more advantageous for the hand.
The Setup:

The game is played with one or more decks of 52 cards.
Each player places a bet before the cards are dealt.
The Deal:

Each player is dealt two cards, usually face up.
The dealer also gets two cards, typically with one face up (the "upcard") and one face down (the "hole card").
Player Decisions:

Hit: Take another card.
Stand: Keep your current hand.
Double Down: Double your bet and receive exactly one more card.
Split: If you have a pair, you can split it into two separate hands, each with its own bet.
Surrender: In some games, you can forfeit half your bet and end your play for the hand.
Dealer Play:

The dealer plays after the players and must hit until their cards total 17 or higher.
Payouts:

If you beat the dealer without busting, you win a 1:1 payout on your bet.
If you get a blackjack (an ace and a 10-point card as your initial two cards), you win a 3:2 payout.
If the dealer busts and you don't, you also win.
Push:

If you and the dealer have the same total, it's a "push," and your bet is returned.
Instructions for Counting Cards:
Card counting is a strategy to track the ratio of high cards to low cards left in the deck. This can give you an idea of the likelihood of receiving a beneficial card. Here's a basic method called the Hi-Lo system:

Assign Values:

Low cards (2-6) are assigned a value of +1.
High cards (10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace) are assigned a value of -1.
Neutral cards (7-9) are assigned a value of 0.
Running Count:

As cards are dealt, keep a "running count" by adding or subtracting according to the values above.
For example, if three low cards are dealt, your running count is +3.
True Count:

To adjust for the number of decks in the game, divide the running count by the approximate number of decks left to be dealt to get the "true count."
This gives you a more accurate picture of the deck's composition.
Bet Sizing:

When the true count is positive, it means there are more high cards left, which favors the player. Increase your bets in these situations.
When the true count is negative, it means there are more low cards left, which favors the dealer. Decrease your bets or bet the minimum.
Playing Decisions:

A positive true count also affects your playing decisions. You may be more inclined to double down, split, or stand on certain hands, depending on the count.
